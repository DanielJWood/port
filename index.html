Bio

Dan has had a life-long love of maps. From an early age it wasn't unusual to find him perusing an atlas for hours on end or passing a long car journey by studying the road map. When given the opportunity to make maps for a summer with the Park Service in Yellowstone, he jumped at it. He hasn't looked back since.


At Mapbox, Dan helped process several hundred thousand records from the US Census Bureau and added about that many points to Open Street Map all over the globe (particularly college campuses. He boned up on his Mapbox JS skills through the help of his genius colleagues and played a few fooseball games too.


Since then, Dan has helped add mapping capacity to Amnesty International USA and worked on a few projects of his own, some serious, and some nonsense. At Edelman, Inc. Dan has had the privilege to learn from talented developers and program managers, working in equal parts between the two positions.


In his free time, Dan is an avid whistler and bicyclist, spending most of his free time doing one or both. Dan likes his bacon medium rare, which he recognizes is unpopular, but he owns, fully.


Specialties: MapboxJs, Tilemill, LeafletJS, QGIS, GDAL, SQL (SQLite, PostgreSQL, PostGIS), Inkscape
